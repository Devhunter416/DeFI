  1. Limited Access:Traditional financial services often require individuals to have a physical presence, a stable income, and a good credit history. This excludes a substantial portion of the world's population, especially those in developing countries or without a formal banking history.
  2. High Fees: Transaction fees imposed by traditional financial institutions can be prohibitively expensive, particularly for small transactions. This makes it challenging for individuals with limited financial resources to engage in economic activities efficiently.
  3. Slow Transaction Times: The conventional financial system is notorious for its bureaucratic processes, resulting in slow transaction times. This not only hampers the efficiency of financial operations but also introduces delays that can be detrimental, especially in time-sensitive situations.
  1. Financial Inclusion:DeFi platforms prioritize inclusivity, allowing anyone with an internet connection to access a range of financial services. This democratization of finance ensures that individuals worldwide can participate in economic activities without facing discriminatory barriers.
  2. Lending and Borrowing:DeFi facilitates peer-to-peer lending and borrowing, enabling users to earn interest on their assets or borrow funds by collateralizing their holdings. This opens up new avenues for individuals to manage their finances and access credit without the need for traditional intermediaries.
  3. Decentralized Exchanges: Traditional exchanges are centralized and prone to issues such as downtime, security breaches, and control by a single entity. DeFi platforms offer decentralized exchanges, allowing users to trade assets directly from their wallets. This reduces reliance on centralized entities and enhances security .
  4. Transparency and Security: Built on blockchain technology, DeFi transactions are transparent, verifiable, and secure. The use of smart contracts ensures that the rules of financial agreements are executed automatically, eliminating the need for intermediaries and reducing the risk of fraud.
